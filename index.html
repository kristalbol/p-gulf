Conflict Snapshot — Day 12 (Dual Escalation + Global Economic Intervention)

Day 12 is a day of simultaneous maximum escalation from both sides and the largest global economic intervention in energy history. The IEA announced a record release of 400 million barrels of oil from emergency reserves — more than double the 2022 Ukraine response — after unanimous agreement from all 32 member nations. IEA Director Birol called the challenges "unprecedented in scale." Yet markets barely flinched: oil rose to ~$91, with analysts calling the release a "water pistol, not a bazooka" against the loss of ~20M bbl/day through Hormuz. Energy Aspects calculates the 400M barrels would be absorbed in just 26 days. One analyst warned: "This conflict needs to end by end of week. Otherwise, oil back over $100."

Both sides escalated overnight. Iran launched its "most intense and heaviest operation" of the entire war — its 37th wave — firing Khoramshahr super-heavy missiles (1+ tonne warheads) at Tel Aviv and Haifa. The IRGC fired on two vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, hitting the Thai-flagged Mayuree Naree (3 crew missing), and 3 more ships were struck off Iran's coast. Two drones fell near Dubai International Airport, injuring 4 and briefly closing airspace. The US/Israel responded with massive overnight strikes on residential Tehran and northern Iran, with the Red Crescent reporting homes hit. CNN confirmed Mojtaba Khamenei suffered a fractured foot and minor injuries from Day 1 strikes — he has not appeared publicly or issued a statement since being named Supreme Leader. A Western intelligence official revealed Russia has escalated from general intel sharing to providing Iran with specific tactical drone advice. Iran's police chief warned protesters would be treated as "enemies." France called an emergency UNSC meeting on Lebanon. WHO warned of toxic black rain health risks. Iran withdrew from the FIFA World Cup, saying its players lack security. Export volumes from the Gulf are at less than 10% of pre-war levels.

Type: Multi-player, incomplete-information, dynamic game with asymmetric payoffs

Structure: Day 12 introduces a Time-Bounded Economic War of Attrition. The IEA's 400M barrel release creates an explicit clock: ~26 days of reserves vs. indefinite Hormuz disruption. Russia's tactical drone assistance deepens the proxy dimension. The game is now simultaneously a military war, an economic war, a proxy war, and a diplomatic crisis — with different time horizons on each dimension.
